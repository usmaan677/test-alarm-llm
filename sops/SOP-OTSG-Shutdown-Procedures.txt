STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURES
SAGD Once-Through Steam Generator (OTSG) and Production Separator
Emergency Shutdown and Alarm Response
Document: SOP-032  |  Revision: 4  |  Classification: Operational - Control Room

=====================================================================
GENERAL NOTES
=====================================================================

All Emergency Shutdown (ESD) actions described in this document are
fail-safe (de-energize to trip). Shutdown keys follow ISA-84 and
IEC 61511 SIL requirements. Operators must acknowledge every alarm on
the HMI and verify the reading against a redundant transmitter before
initiating any manual shutdown action unless an automatic ESD has
already triggered.

ESD Levels are defined as:
  ESD Level 1 - Plant-wide shutdown
  ESD Level 2 - Unit shutdown (affects a single process unit)
  ESD Level 3 - Loop or well shutdown (most localized)

=====================================================================
SOP-032-A : OTSG STEAM DRUM PRESSURE VERY HIGH
=====================================================================

Alarm Tag:        PAHH-101
Equipment:        Once-Through Steam Generator (OTSG)
Trip Condition:   Steam drum pressure greater than 11,500 kPag
ESD Level:        Level 2 (Unit)

Required Action:
  1. Acknowledge the PAHH-101 alarm on the control room HMI.
  2. Verify the pressure reading against the redundant pressure
     transmitter PT-101B.
  3. If pressure is confirmed above 11,500 kPag, shut the OTSG.
  4. Close steam shutdown valves SDV-101 and SDV-102.
  5. Confirm both SDVs have reached the fully closed position.
  6. Notify the shift supervisor and log the event with timestamp.
  7. Do not restart the OTSG until the root cause has been identified
     and the high-pressure condition has cleared.

=====================================================================
SOP-032-B : OTSG STACK TEMPERATURE VERY HIGH
=====================================================================

Alarm Tag:        TAHH-101
Equipment:        OTSG Stack
Trip Condition:   Stack temperature greater than 400 degrees C
ESD Level:        Level 3 (Loop)

Required Action:
  1. Acknowledge the TAHH-101 alarm on the HMI.
  2. Trip the OTSG burner immediately.
  3. Close the fuel gas shutdown valve SDV-FG-101.
  4. Verify burner flame-out via the burner management system.
  5. Notify the shift supervisor and log the event.
  6. Inspect for fuel gas valve leak-by before any restart attempt.

=====================================================================
SOP-032-C : FWKO SEPARATOR LEVEL VERY HIGH
=====================================================================

Alarm Tag:        LAHH-201
Equipment:        Free Water Knockout (FWKO) Separator
Trip Condition:   Liquid level greater than 85 percent
ESD Level:        Level 3 (Loop)

Required Action:
  1. Acknowledge the LAHH-201 alarm on the HMI.
  2. Close the production inlet to the separator.
  3. Stop the Electric Submersible Pump (ESP).
  4. Confirm separator level begins to fall.
  5. Notify the shift supervisor and log the event.

=====================================================================
SOP-032-D : FWKO SEPARATOR LEVEL VERY LOW
=====================================================================

Alarm Tag:        LALL-201
Equipment:        Free Water Knockout (FWKO) Separator
Trip Condition:   Liquid level less than 10 percent
ESD Level:        Level 3 (Loop)

Required Action:
  1. Acknowledge the LALL-201 alarm on the HMI.
  2. Close the oil outlet valve to prevent gas blow-by downstream.
  3. Raise the low-level alarm to the shift supervisor.
  4. Investigate inlet flow loss before resetting.

=====================================================================
SOP-032-E : FWKO SEPARATOR PRESSURE VERY HIGH
=====================================================================

Alarm Tag:        PAHH-201
Equipment:        Free Water Knockout (FWKO) Separator
Trip Condition:   Pressure greater than 1,200 kPag
ESD Level:        Level 3 (Well)

Required Action:
  1. Acknowledge the PAHH-201 alarm on the HMI.
  2. Confirm pressure safety valve PSV-201 has opened to relieve.
  3. Close the inlet shutdown valve SDV-201.
  4. Monitor separator pressure until it returns below 1,200 kPag.
  5. Notify the shift supervisor and log the event.

=====================================================================
SOP-032-F : ESP MOTOR TEMPERATURE VERY HIGH
=====================================================================

Alarm Tag:        TAHH-ESP
Equipment:        Electric Submersible Pump (ESP) Motor
Trip Condition:   Motor temperature greater than 220 degrees C
ESD Level:        Level 3 (Well)

Required Action:
  1. Acknowledge the TAHH-ESP alarm on the HMI.
  2. Trip the ESP.
  3. Close the well shutdown valve SDV-201.
  4. Allow the motor to cool before any restart attempt.
  5. Notify the shift supervisor and log the event.

=====================================================================
SOP-032-G : INJECTION WELLHEAD TEMPERATURE VERY LOW
=====================================================================

Alarm Tag:        TALL-INJ
Equipment:        Injection Wellhead
Trip Condition:   Temperature less than 100 degrees C
ESD Level:        Level 3 (Well)

Required Action:
  1. Acknowledge the TALL-INJ alarm on the HMI.
  2. Close the injection shutdown valve SDV-101.
  3. Raise the low-temperature alarm to the shift supervisor.
  4. Investigate steam supply loss to the injection wellhead.
